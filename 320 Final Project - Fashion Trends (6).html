I. INTRODUCTION & MOTIVATION:

The purpose of this project is to outline the steps of the data science pipeline. To demonstrate this, I will be analyzing google search trends for outfit-related google searches over the past year, 5 years, and 10 years. I chose to analyze google searches with the phrase "outfits with" to provide the largest set of data related to by desired research. I tried a variety of different search phrases including "how to style", "styling", "how to wear", etc... and found that the "outfits with" search phrase provided that most relevant information.

The purpose of this data collection is to identify clothing items that will maintain relevance in the long-run, and what groups of individuals are most profitable to market to. Why is this analysis helpful? Let's say you would like to become a small business owner of an online boutique. In order to ensure the clothes you carry do not fall out of trend before they are able to be sold, focus should be placed on clothing items that will maintain relevance and popularity for years to come. Maybe you are more focused on the sustainability of items you carry (using recycled polyesters, stainless steel, etc..). It would then also be in your best interest to advertise clothing that you could advertise as timeless, staple pieces in a wardrobe such as jeans and a white cotton tee shirt. This data would also be useful in targeted ads.

The above table shows data available for previous 10 years. The second column includes data for "top searches" and "rising searches". Google defines these terms as the following:

TOP SEARCHES - The most popular search queries. Scoring is on a relative scale where a value of 100 is the most commonly searched query, 50 is a query searched half as often as the most popular query, and so on.

RISING SEARCHES - Queries with the biggest increase in search frequency since the last time period. Results marked "Breakout" had a tremendous increase, probably because these queries are new and had few (if any) prior searches.
